Hiroshima Okonomiyaki

Home

Description

Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki is a Japanese savory pancake with cabbage, bean sprout, noodles, sliced pork belly, and a fried egg, topped with savory sauce and Japanese mayo.

Ingredients

  • Batter:
    • 2/3 cup water (measure 2/3 & remove 2 tsp to be precise)
    • 1 tsp mirin
    • 3/4 cup AP flour (3/4 cup +1 1/2 tbsp to be precise; use cake flour if have)
  • 8.5 oz green cabbage
  • 1 scallion
  • 2 tbsp katsuobushi/bonito flakes (grind to powder)
  • 4 tbsp tempura scraps (tenkasu/agedama)
  • 2 oz bean sprouts
  • 2 tbsp tororo kombu (optional)
  • 12 slices pork belly
  • 2 servings yakisoba noodles
  • 2 large eggs
  • Toppings:
    • okonomiyaki sauce
    • kewpie mayo
    • aonori

Steps

  1. In a medium-large bowl, add water & mirin. Add flour & whisk until combined. Chill batter for ~1 hour.
  2. Gather the rest of the ingredients. Thinly slice cabbage & scallion. Grind katsuobushi into a fine powder.
  3. Heat a large non-stick griddle/skillet and oil it. Add 1/4 cup of batter, and move the ladle in a spiral motion from the center towards the edge to make a circle.
  4. Sprinkle katsuobushi on batter & place cabbage, tenkasu, scallion, & beansprout on top.
  5. Put tororo kombu & pork belly.
  6. Pour 1 tbsp of batter on top. Using 2 spatulas on each side, flip. Turn the heat up to cook the meat. When the pork isn’t pink, turn the heat down.
  7. Heat up another pan with oil. Stir-fry the yakisoba noodles until coated with oil. Add 1-2 tbsp of okonomiyaki sauce & coat the noodles.
  8. Make the noodles into a round shape & transfer the okonomiyaki on top of the noodles.
  9. Heat oil in pan again & crack an egg. Quickly spread the egg until it’s the same size as okonomiyaki.
  10. Before egg is completely cooked, place okonomiyaki on top of egg.
  11. Using 2 spatulas, flip okonomiyaki one last time.
  12. Drizzle okonomi sauce, mayo, & aonori.
